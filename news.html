About the Lab

Mission, research focus, and recent activities.

Our Mission

The Smart & Sustainable Transportation Lab (SSTL) is dedicated to advancing research and developing innovative solutions for modern mobility challenges. Our work focuses on the intersection of transportation engineering, artificial intelligence, and sustainable urban planning.

We are a dynamic research group based at the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Université Laval. Our primary goal is to address complex transportation issues through rigorous data-driven analysis, advanced modeling techniques, and the development of cutting-edge technologies. We aim to create transportation systems that are not only efficient and intelligent but also equitable and environmentally sustainable.

Our Approach

Our research integrates theoretical insights with practical, data-driven decision modeling. We specialize in several key areas:

  • Analysis of travel behavior and discrete choice modeling.
  • Development of integrated urban mobility solutions like Mobility as a Service (MaaS).
  • Examination of the relationship between transportation, climate change, and land use.
  • Application of AI, machine learning, and econometric modeling to solve transportation problems.

The lab is led by Prof. Bobin Wang, a leading expert in transportation analysis and modeling. Our team is composed of passionate graduate students and researchers who are committed to making a positive impact on the future of mobility.

Conference October 2025 Québec, Canada

Transportation Association of Canada (TAC Conference)

SSTL participated in the 2025 Transportation Association of Canada (TAC) Annual Conference, the largest national gathering of transportation professionals in Canada. The conference brings together experts from industry, government (municipal, provincial, territorial, and federal), universities, and professional associations, offering a rich program of technical sessions, panels, workshops, and an exhibition showcasing the latest innovations in transportation.

During TAC 2025, our team contributed through multiple presentations highlighting ongoing research projects, as well as participation in panel sessions and poster sessions. This event provided an excellent opportunity to share our work, exchange ideas with the broader transportation community, and strengthen connections with partners working on real-world mobility challenges.

Lab life September 2025

SSTL Visual Identity — Logo Creation

To build a strong and consistent visual identity for SSTL, we organized an internal design competition to select the lab’s official logo and color palette. Team members reviewed multiple proposals and voted for the combination that best represents our mission in smart and sustainable transportation.

Following the selection, we applied the new identity across our communication materials and produced branded merchandise—including SSTL t-shirts and caps—to support outreach activities, conference participation, and lab visibility. This initiative helped strengthen our team culture while ensuring a professional and recognizable presence at events.

Social activities

Beyond research, SSTL values a strong and supportive team culture. Throughout the year, we organize social activities such as sports matches, team dinners, and group gatherings that create space to connect, recharge, and celebrate milestones together. These moments strengthen collaboration, encourage open communication, and help maintain an inclusive environment where everyone can thrive—both academically and personally.
