Integrated Multimodal Travel Behavior Analysis under Mobility as a Service

Objective

This project develops a comprehensive framework to analyze and model traveler behavior in a multimodal transportation system integrated under the Mobility as a Service (MaaS) paradigm. We investigate how individuals make travel choices when presented with a seamless digital platform that combines multiple modes—public transit, ride-sharing, bike rentals, and emerging services.

Methodology

Our approach combines stated preference surveys, real-world travel diary data, and advanced discrete choice modeling. By leveraging data-driven techniques (including machine learning when appropriate), we identify key determinants of mode choice, route selection, and activity scheduling within a MaaS ecosystem. The models account for travel time, cost, convenience, real-time information, and attitudes toward sustainability and technology.

Expected Outcomes

The primary outcome is a robust behavioral model to support policymakers and operators in designing more effective, user-centric, and sustainable MaaS services. The results will help predict adoption rates, assess impacts on congestion and emissions, and optimize service integration to maximize societal benefits.
