The potential of battery electric trucks in forest transportation

Objective

This project aims to develop a comprehensive framework for analyzing and modeling traveler behavior in a multimodal transportation system integrated under the Mobility as a Service (MaaS) paradigm. We investigate how individuals make travel choices when presented with a seamless digital platform that combines various transport modes, from public transit and ride-sharing to bike rentals and autonomous shuttles.

Methodology

Our approach combines stated preference surveys, real-world travel diary data, and advanced discrete choice modeling techniques. By leveraging machine learning algorithms, we aim to identify the key determinants of mode choice, route selection, and activity scheduling within a MaaS ecosystem. The model will account for factors such as travel time, cost, convenience, real-time information, and personal attitudes towards sustainability and technology.

Expected Outcomes

The primary outcome will be a robust behavioral model that can be used by policymakers and transport operators to design more effective, user-centric, and sustainable urban mobility systems. This model will help predict the adoption rates of new MaaS services, assess their impact on congestion and emissions, and optimize service integration for maximum societal benefit.
